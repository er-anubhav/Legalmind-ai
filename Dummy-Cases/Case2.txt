Case No.: CS(COMM) 2024/01532

Judge: Hon. Justice Neha Deshmukh

POST-HEARING CASE SUMMARY

Date of Final Hearing: August 21-25, 2024

Matter: Breach of Contract and Intellectual Property Infringement

I. CASE OVERVIEW
This case involves a commercial dispute between Mumbai Textiles Pvt. Ltd. ("Mumbai Textiles") and FashionEdge Retail Ltd. ("FashionEdge") regarding an alleged breach of a supply agreement and infringement of copyrighted textile designs.

II. PARTIES
Plaintiff: Mumbai Textiles Pvt. Ltd.

A private limited company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its registered office in Mumbai, Maharashtra.
Represented by: Desai & Shah Legal Associates
Defendant: FashionEdge Retail Ltd.

A public limited company incorporated under the Companies Act, 1956, with its head office in Bengaluru, Karnataka.
Represented by: Kapoor & Partners Law Firm
III. FACTUAL BACKGROUND
The Supply Agreement:
On March 1, 2023, Mumbai Textiles entered into a supply agreement with FashionEdge to provide exclusive textile designs for their upcoming clothing line. The agreement contained clauses regarding the use of designs and protection of intellectual property.

Alleged Breach:
Mumbai Textiles alleges that FashionEdge violated the agreement by manufacturing garments using their designs without proper authorization and failing to pay the agreed royalties. The plaintiff claims this breach has led to significant financial and reputational damage.

Intellectual Property Infringement:
In addition to the breach of contract, Mumbai Textiles contends that FashionEdge infringed on copyrighted textile designs by replicating them in their product line without permission, resulting in unfair competition.

IV. ISSUES PRESENTED
Whether FashionEdge breached the supply agreement with Mumbai Textiles.
Whether FashionEdge infringed on Mumbai Textiles' copyrighted designs.
Whether Mumbai Textiles is entitled to damages and injunctive relief.
V. ARGUMENTS
1. Plaintiff's Arguments:
Mumbai Textiles argues that FashionEdge knowingly breached the agreement by using their designs without authorization and failed to pay the royalties as stipulated in the contract.
The plaintiff further claims that the infringement of copyrighted designs has caused substantial losses, both financially and in terms of market reputation.
Mumbai Textiles seeks damages for the breach and an injunction to prevent FashionEdge from continuing to use their designs.
2. Defendant's Arguments:
FashionEdge denies the breach, stating that the designs were legally purchased and that they have fulfilled all contractual obligations.
The defendant also argues that the designs in question are not unique enough to qualify for copyright protection and that Mumbai Textiles is attempting to monopolize common design elements.
FashionEdge seeks dismissal of the case and recovery of legal expenses.
VI. JUDGMENT RESERVED
The court has reserved its judgment, with a decision expected by October 2024.
