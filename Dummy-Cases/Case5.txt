Case No.: CS(COMM) 2024/02147

Judge: Hon. Justice Renu Mehra

POST-HEARING CASE SUMMARY

Date of Final Hearing: September 15-19, 2024

Matter: Breach of Service Agreement and Data Privacy Violation

I. CASE OVERVIEW
This case involves a commercial dispute between SecureIT Solutions Pvt. Ltd. ("SecureIT") and Vortex Technologies Ltd. ("Vortex") regarding an alleged breach of a service agreement and unauthorized access to confidential data.

II. PARTIES
Plaintiff: SecureIT Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

A private limited company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its head office in New Delhi, specializing in cybersecurity services.
Represented by: Khanna & Kapoor Advocates
Defendant: Vortex Technologies Ltd.

A public limited company incorporated under the Companies Act, 1956, with its registered office in Pune, Maharashtra, specializing in cloud computing and IT infrastructure services.
Represented by: Malhotra & Partners Law Firm
III. FACTUAL BACKGROUND
The Service Agreement:
On January 10, 2023, SecureIT entered into a service agreement with Vortex to provide cybersecurity solutions for Vortex's cloud-based platform. The agreement included clauses regarding data protection, confidentiality, and service-level commitments.

Alleged Breach:
SecureIT alleges that Vortex breached the agreement by failing to implement the recommended security measures, leading to unauthorized access and data leaks from the cloud platform. The plaintiff also claims that Vortex accessed SecureIT's proprietary security algorithms without authorization, violating confidentiality terms.

Data Privacy Violation:
In addition to the breach of contract, SecureIT contends that Vortex's actions led to a significant data privacy violation, compromising sensitive information of multiple clients and damaging SecureIT's reputation in the cybersecurity industry.

IV. ISSUES PRESENTED
Whether Vortex breached the service agreement with SecureIT.
Whether Vortex violated data privacy laws and confidentiality obligations.
Whether SecureIT is entitled to damages and injunctive relief.
V. ARGUMENTS
1. Plaintiff's Arguments:
SecureIT argues that Vortex's failure to implement adequate security measures directly resulted in data breaches that have caused severe financial and reputational damage to SecureIT and its clients.
The plaintiff further contends that Vortex's unauthorized access to proprietary security algorithms constitutes a violation of confidentiality clauses in the agreement, warranting both compensatory and punitive damages.
SecureIT seeks financial compensation for damages and an injunction to prevent further unauthorized use of its proprietary information.
2. Defendant's Arguments:
Vortex denies the breach, asserting that SecureIT's security recommendations were insufficient to prevent the data breaches and that the unauthorized access was due to external factors beyond their control.
The defendant also argues that the use of SecureIT's algorithms was within the scope of the service agreement and that no confidentiality terms were violated.
Vortex seeks dismissal of the case and recovery of legal costs.
VI. JUDGMENT RESERVED
The court has reserved its judgment, with a decision expected by November 2024.

VII. RELIEF SOUGHT
Compensatory Damages: ₹50 crore for financial losses and damage to business reputation.
Punitive Damages: ₹25 crore for willful violation of data privacy and confidentiality.
Injunctive Relief: Permanent injunction preventing Vortex from using SecureIT's proprietary algorithms and any further unauthorized access to confidential information.
