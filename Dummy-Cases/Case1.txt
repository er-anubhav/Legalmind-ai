Case No.: CS(COMM) 2024/00987

Judge: Hon. Justice Rajesh Kumar

POST-HEARING CASE SUMMARY

Date of Final Hearing: July 10-14, 2024

Matter: Breach of Contract and Misappropriation of Trade Secrets

I. CASE OVERVIEW
This case involves a commercial dispute between Bharat Solar Ltd. ("Bharat Solar") and Sunrise Energy Pvt. Ltd. ("Sunrise Energy") regarding an alleged breach of a joint development agreement and misappropriation of trade secrets related to advanced solar energy technology.

II. PARTIES
Plaintiff: Bharat Solar Ltd.

A company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its principal place of business in New Delhi, India.
Represented by: Mehta & Associates Advocates
Defendant: Sunrise Energy Pvt. Ltd.

A private limited company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its registered office in Gurugram, Haryana.
Represented by: Gupta & Partners Law Firm
III. FACTUAL BACKGROUND
The Joint Development Agreement:
On January 15, 2022, Bharat Solar and Sunrise Energy entered into a joint development agreement to collaborate on the research and development of solar panel technology. The agreement included provisions for sharing intellectual property and protecting trade secrets.

Alleged Breach:
Bharat Solar alleges that Sunrise Energy breached the agreement by unlawfully disclosing confidential information to third-party competitors. Specifically, Bharat Solar claims that proprietary designs and technology were shared without consent, resulting in financial losses.

Misappropriation of Trade Secrets:
In addition to the breach of contract, Bharat Solar contends that Sunrise Energy misappropriated trade secrets related to a breakthrough solar panel prototype, using the information to gain a competitive advantage in the market.

IV. ISSUES PRESENTED
Whether Sunrise Energy breached the joint development agreement.
Whether Sunrise Energy misappropriated trade secrets belonging to Bharat Solar.
Whether Bharat Solar is entitled to damages and injunctive relief.
V. ARGUMENTS
1. Plaintiff's Arguments:
Bharat Solar argues that Sunrise Energy violated the terms of the joint development agreement by disclosing confidential information.
The plaintiff further contends that the misappropriation of trade secrets caused irreparable harm to its business and reputation.
Bharat Solar seeks compensatory and punitive damages, as well as a permanent injunction to prevent further disclosure of its trade secrets.
2. Defendant's Arguments:
Sunrise Energy denies any breach of contract, claiming that the information shared was not covered under the agreement's confidentiality clause.
The defendant also argues that the alleged trade secrets were not sufficiently protected by Bharat Solar, and therefore cannot be considered proprietary.
Sunrise Energy seeks dismissal of the case and reimbursement of legal costs.
VI. JUDGMENT RESERVED
The court has reserved its judgment, with a decision expected by September 2024.

VII. RELIEF SOUGHT
Compensatory Damages: ₹50 crore for financial losses incurred by Bharat Solar.
Punitive Damages: ₹10 crore for the willful misappropriation of trade secrets.
Injunctive Relief: Permanent injunction preventing Sunrise Energy from using or disclosing any trade secrets belonging to Bharat Solar.
